Nogfx is back!

After a looong hiatus, we’re finally back. This time with some extras.

The first version of this website saw the light of day back in 2006. It was a very simple piece of software, where you could upload a log from your MUD gaming session, to more easily share your experience.

Kind of like a geekier predecessor to Twitch. (Except, for some reason, Amazon never offered me a nine-figure acquisition offer…)

Over the years I kept adding features – character verification, voting, commenting, etc. It was still all very basic, as is evident from these old screenshots of how it looked up until this year:

Design has never really been my forte but I'm happy with the improvement!

Regardless of the crappy looks and half-working features, people seemed to like the website. As time went by, together we managed to share more than 1500 logs and create over 3000 comments.

Then, life happened and I no longer had the time to play MUDs. While Nogfx kept living its own life, over time, stuff broke down and stopped working and the website saw an ever decreasing usage.

MUDding has a special place in my heart, though, having cut my teeth on coding for them, which turned into my professional career. I’ve got so many fond memories from playing MUDs, meeting people from all over the world, and building something together.

Finding myself with a tiny bit of extra spare time recently, I decided to breathe new life into this project, revamping the website and hopefully making it even more useful for our community.

New design

As you might have noticed, this revamp started with a redesign of the website. I’m by no means a good designer but at least I’m better than I was one and a half decade ago, evident by the above screenshots.

This new version of Nogfx supports everything essential from the old one, apart from some key features. Like adding new logs, votes, and comments. Anything that requires a costly database, really.

You can still add to it through the open GitHub repo but I’ll make this work better, so you can upload your logs and discuss them directly here on the website. Plus, I’ve got some ideas for how to take the logs archive to a whole new level but that’ll be a surprise.

One other thing that’s already vastly improved in this version is the load times. It used to take roughly two seconds for every page to load; over ten with the logs list. Reload this page and see the difference.
